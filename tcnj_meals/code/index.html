    TCNJ Meals Food Bank Welcomes You to Our Site!

    ABOUT

    TCNJ Meals Food Bank is one of the few local food pantries in Mercer County, but this one in particular serves our very own students struggling with food insecurity. This pantry receives donations from dining halls around campus and from private donors. If you would like to receive food assistance from TCNJ Meals but have mobility issues, please email tcnjmeals@gmail.com to schedule an appointment. We will be happy to meet with you and make arrangements and accommodations for a more suitable location for your food pickup.


    Food Insecurity Aricle

    Food insecurity, which is not having reliable, available quantities of food, is apparent in TCNJ, even though many students may not visibly see it. In April of 2018, Heather Haase, Web Editor of TCNJ Signal, wrote an article surrounding the food insecurity issue TCNJ students struggle with. She noted a survey conducted by another university in which more than a third of college students have been food insecure within the past 30 days. As students, your close friends may not know where their next meal will come from, and you may not even have a clue. As professors, your studentsâ€™ greatest concerns sometimes are not about their grades, but rather about survival. Although there are meal plans at TCNJ, they cost thousands of dollars a year to have, which is not a viable option for every student. Thus, there is a need for resources that aid students who do not have enough money for food. TCNJ Meals Food Bank serves to lessen the food insecurity at TCNJ.
